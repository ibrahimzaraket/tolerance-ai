Step 2 ‚Äî Relaunch
Stress-testing ideas with WWHWWW questions
We took our strongest ideas and challenged each one through the Who, What, How, Where, When, Why lens to uncover blind spots and refine them.
Who benefits most?
Young professionals (22‚Äì35) in metro areas who feel guilty about wasted time and are motivated by career growth. Secondary: students preparing for exams, employers investing in team skills.
What format works?
Must be modular and interruptible ‚Äî a lesson should pause and resume without losing progress. Mix of visual (flashcards), audio (guided), and interactive (quizzes) depending on context.
How do we keep them coming back?
Habit loops: trigger (commute start), routine (open app), reward (XP + streak). Push notifications at departure time. Social proof via leaderboards. Loss aversion through streak protection.
Where does it fail?
Short commutes (under 10 min) ‚Äî content must have a "quick mode." Rural areas with no transit. Users who drive. Solution: audio-only mode for drivers, ultra-short 3-min sessions.
When is engagement highest?
Morning commutes see higher learning engagement (fresh mind). Evening commutes favor lighter content ‚Äî reflections, summaries, casual quizzes. Weekends drop off ‚Äî need streak-freeze mechanics.
Why would they choose us over scrolling?
Because we make progress visible and rewarding. After 30 days, you've completed a module. After 90, a certification. Social media gives dopamine but no lasting value ‚Äî we give both.
